Fiction Picks

The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins

The Hunger Games is a gripping novel set in a post-apocalyptic future in the nation of Panem, where a powerful Capitol controls twelve poorer districts. Each year, the Capitol forces one boy and one girl from each district (ages 12–18) to fight to the death in a televised event known as The Hunger Games.

The story follows Katniss Everdeen, a brave 16-year-old from District 12, who volunteers to take her younger sister’s place in the deadly competition. Told from Katniss’s point of view, the novel explores themes of survival, government control, sacrifice, and resistance.

The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho

The Alchemist is a philosophical novel about Santiago, a young shepherd from Spain who dreams of finding treasure near the Egyptian pyramids. He sets off on a life-changing journey across the desert, guided by signs, a king, and a wise alchemist.

Along the way, he falls in love and learns powerful lessons about destiny, self-discovery, and the soul of the world. Ultimately, Santiago realizes that true treasure lies within and at home.

Non-Fiction Picks

Born a Crime by Trevor Noah

Born a Crime is Trevor Noah’s powerful and humorous memoir about growing up mixed-race in apartheid and post-apartheid South Africa. Born to a black mother and white father when their relationship was illegal, Trevor navigates poverty, racism, and abuse with resilience and wit.

At its heart, the book honors his fearless mother, Patricia, whose love and faith shape his life. Through personal stories, Noah reflects on identity, injustice, survival, and the power of laughter.

The Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank

The Diary of a Young Girl is the powerful, real-life diary of Anne Frank, a Jewish teenager who hid with her family for two years during the Nazi occupation of the Netherlands. Through her words, Anne shares deep thoughts, daily struggles, and hopes for the future.

After their arrest, Anne died in a concentration camp. Her father, Otto, later published her diary, which became one of the most important records of the Holocaust.
